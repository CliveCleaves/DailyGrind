Daily Grind
An unlikely group of people find themselves coping with the daily grind of life at their local coffee shop in this multigenerational comedy.

Episode 1

Episode Guide

Pilot - The Daily Grind ...Literally

Kara is fed up with her job. She has an education and is way better than working at a coffee shop, in her opinion. She rants to Pastor Jon who comes in early before other customers. He encourages her to look beyond herself. In a series of humorous events, she encounters a sweet moment. At the end of the episode she begins a written commentary on the day’s events, could a change of heart be starting?

Episode 2 - Can you Claim that?

Frank is furious when a random customer keeps taking his seat in the morning. He is infuriated by the issue and even takes his problem to Management. Will his constant attempt to claim back his spot in the cafe succeed?

Episode 3 - Something doesn’t look right.

Henri is a new police officer in town and Nikki is very starry eyed. She loves a man in uniform! When she discovers he carries the smallest feminine looking change purse she takes it upon herself to make sure he has one that suits his job. Will Henri appreciate the gesture, or will it fail miserably and put his safety at risk?

Episode 4 - Avoiding the Sting

The fall can be a terrible time for wasps in an area of the world with seasons. Kara does a garbage run and quickly discovers the challenge these wasps create. She recruits help from Nikki to deal with the problem when they’re mission quickly fails and they find themselves calling pest-control. Pest control teaches them a lesson they didn’t see coming.

Episode 5 - My Superman

Kara finds a superman child’s wallet but doesn’t recognize the photo ID, as the gentleman has a mullet and mustache. She spends the day trying to find the owner and is shocked when she discovers the real owner of the wallet. Her and Nikki quickly learn to never judge a person by their wallet as they have a habit of doing.

Episode 6 - Tying the knot

When an unknown customer comes into the store to purchase tea, Kara is quickly bothered by the encounter as it is taking everything in her not to lose it! Nikki quickly comes to help with the difficult customer and soon the whole cafe is aware of what is happening. Will Kara be able to get past this, or will she reach her breaking point?

Episode 7 - Dan the Delivery Man

Kara and Nikki arrive at the store first thing in the morning to find their order boxes practically blocking the door, they scramble to get the boxes out of the way before the customers arrive but are unsuccessful. Drew is forced to try and convince Dan the delivery man, who is new to delivering at their store, to put the boxes against the wall. After many failed attempts to convince Dan by the various staff, the person who ends up convincing Dan, shocks everyone.

Episode 8 - Stuff the Turkey

With Thanksgiving around the corner, the staff at the Morning Jolt, want to give back to the community. They develop a plan to do a fundraiser for their local foodbank. Dustin uses his artistic skills to make a turkey box to hold all the donations. The turkey is just not getting stuffed at a rate that the staff thought it would. Racking their brains, they devise a plan to see the turkey get stuffed before Thanksgiving passes, does their plan work?

Episode 9 - Leaving the safety zone

Kara is starting to really enjoy going to work, as her perspective has started to change. It’s just some things are still really bugging her. Every times she leaves from behind the counter into the cafe, she gets hounded by customers for various reasons. She brings her frustrations to Drew, who at first doesn’t believe it is that bad. After Kara articulates the challenges Drew ends up with the same problem. Drew decides to call an emergency staff meeting, how will they protect the staff when they leave the safety zone?

Episode 10 - A toothy grin

The staff observe that Frank has never smiled or laughed in the entirety of them knowing him. It is starting to really bother the staff as they are concerned that he is really unhappy. The team make a pact to try and make him laugh. They are pulling out all the stops and while everyone else seems to laugh, Frank is not cracking! Will anyone be able to make Frank laugh?

Episode 11 - Nativity?

The staff hold their annual christmas meeting to prepare for the busy season. It is one of their favourite times of year to spread holiday cheer. They establish a well crafted calendar of events. It is also tradition for the local community church to hold a nativity performance just outside the coffee shop and Kara is responsible for overseeing the details. A debate breaks out whether it is okay to say “Merry Christmas” and if the nativity is appropriate? WIll the community be able to come to an agreement?

Episode 12 - Life as an Elf

At the Christmas staff meeting the team had agreed that on December 24th, they would all dress up as elves. The 24th has arrived and one staff member fully commits to the agreement. Unfortunately the pact has gone all wrong and one staff member gets to experience life as a lone elf.

Characters

Principal Characters - Staff

Kara is early 20’s and mixed first nations and caucasian. She has wanted to be an actress for as long as she can remember but ended up with a job at a coffee shop. She has an arts degree and she often auditions. Drew is very accommodating whenever she needs extra time off. She has yet to book an acting job but everyone of her customers thinks she’s going to be a star. She is very friendly and way too efficient at the store.

Nikki is in her 30’s and caucasian. She is overweight and ditzy. She is very outgoing and is desperate to find a man which results in her being overly flirtatious with good looking customers. Specifically officers, because she loves a man in uniform. She is always dressed fashionably and her hair is done in funky ways. Nikki’s make-up is usually perfect her favourite lip colour is red!

Drew is in his early 30’s, he is caucasian with blond hair and blue eyes. He is the coffee shop manager and he has a chill surfer quality about him. He has some facial hair and is very relaxed. He is quick on his feet and smart, though he comes across as simple. Very likable and has a really big crush and Kara, but he doesn’t let on, as he feels there is no chance. It makes him agree with any of Kara’s demands.

Principal Characters - Customers

Henri is mid to late 20’s and just over 6'. He is of french descent with dark hair and dark features. He is a new police officer in town. He is fairly reserved and is always aware of his surroundings. He takes his job as an officer very seriously and takes great concern for the well being of the community. When he sees Kara for the first time, he is completely floored by her beauty. They never seem to cross paths long enough to have a word. Seth and Nikki are usually running the conversations.

Frank is in his 70’s and european. He is quiet and grumpy and prefers to drink his coffee in peace. He is mid-height and not overweight, but has a slight belly. He drinks a tall black bold which is very reflective of his view on life. Simple with no unnecessary add-ons. Frank is not one to smile, he is VERY serious.

Crystal is in her late 20’s and is mixed ethnicity, she has four kids. Two are in school, and two are still home with her. When she comes in she has the newborn and her three year old daughter Kate. She is obsessed with everything from the Royal family, and even named her daughter after Kate Middleton. She has a sweet demeanor but can seem very frazzled because her children make it difficult for her to do very simple things.

Ron (Ronald) is late 50’s he is caucasian and married to Pamela, they are both recently retired. Ron is mild and jolly, he loves to make a good Dad joke when the opportunity presents itself. He is finding it hard to adjust to retirement as he likes to be busy with a clear purpose.

Pam (Pamela) is mid 50s and Asian American. She is retired and married to Ron. Her last child has left for university and she is trying to cope with having an empty nest. She also has the additional challenge of having Ron home all the time.

Supporting Characters - Staff

Dustin is in his early 20’s and is asian american. He was just hired at the coffee shop as he was desperate for a job. He finished college and has a diploma in graphic design but hasn’t been able to get a job in his field. He is an introvert and doesn’t make great choices when it comes to customer service.

Ruby is in her teens, Ruby is still in high school. She works part-time and on holidays. She has a youthful exuberance about her. Quiet, kind and sometimes a bit of a pushover. Can be very gullible. She doesn’t catch onto sarcasm.

Dan is a new delivery man to the coffee shop. He is stuck in his ways and is not one to make changes based on recommendations. He guest stars in one episode but also appears here and there as his job calls for it.

Carlos is the pest control consultant for the coffee shop. He was born in latin america and immigrated to the U.S. as an adult. He is a hard worker, loves to chat and very likable. He guest stars in an episode, and again we see him periodically through the season.

Supporting Characters - Customers

Jon (Jonathan) is in his 40’s and is African American. He is the pastor at the local community church. He is very mild, he is quick to listen and is a man of few words, until the time calls for it. He comes into the coffee shop to write sermons and visit with members of the community and his congregation. He sometimes has counseling sessions early in the mornings.

George is in his 80’s and is caucasian. He is a short and fit elderly man. He is a retired business owner who is wealthy and opinionated. He drinks green tea every day to keep healthy. His hearing is starting to fade, so when he speaks he is very loud and he laughs easily. Stuck in his ways, he never orders because he expects the staff to know his drink right away.

Seth is mid 30’s and African American. He is taller than Henri and very stocky. He is very chatty and loves to crack jokes that he thinks are funny. He relishes in the fact he is a police officer and tries to use it to impress the ladies. Though he talks a lot, he is a genuinely kind and caring man who really just wants to find love. He is very sweet on Nikki, though she is completely oblivious to it, because she has her sight set on Henri.

Catherine (Kate) - Crystals 3 year old is super chatty and outgoing. Source of entertainment for the older customers.

Alex - Crystal’s baby

About Me

Charline Cleaver graduated from Queens University with a Bachelor of Arts in Drama and Music as well as a Bachelor of Education in the same fields. She worked at a coffee shop for a few years following the completion of her degree while she pursued her dream of working in the film and television industry. She received a request to work at a Model and Talent Agency and was excited by the prospect! She loved everyone at the coffee shop and found the transition difficult. Over the course of the year, she started to see that she was not a match for working at a secular agency and left, trusting that God had a plan for her life. She fell into working as a background actor in Toronto. It was exciting for her to be on the sets of network television shows and feature films giving her insight into the filming process. She takes every opportunity to learn as much as she can while on set. In her downtime she loves to create and write. Charline holds tightly to her values and faith in Jesus. The journey has not been easy for her. Being a christian in an industry that often seems so counter to God has presented it’s challenges. She’s had to figure out how to maneuver the business and how to have the courage to say no, while trying to remain a positive witness. It has created a longing for roles that people who love the Lord can be confident portraying. Messages that will help and not hinder. The more she has worked, the more she has seen the need for wholesome, family friendly entertainment. All the experiences and hardships have led to the stories on the pages. Charline loves to pull from real life experiences and from people who have inspired her through the years. Her dream is to someday work on a creative team bringing to life stories that inspire the human heart.
